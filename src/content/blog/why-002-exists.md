The consulting market has two tiers.

At the top: big firms that charge enterprise rates, send in a rotating cast of junior consultants, and produce a lot of slides. At the bottom: managed-service providers that want you on a monthly retainer and will put a ticket queue between you and anyone who can actually fix your problem.

Almost nothing sits cleanly in the middle — one experienced person who'll look at what you have, tell you the truth about it, and then do the work. That's the space 002 Solutions is trying to occupy.

## What "senior-level, scoped engagement" actually means

Three things, in practice:

1. **You talk to the person doing the work.** No account manager, no junior handoff, no "let me loop in someone from our delivery team." If we're working together, we're working together.

2. **The scope is written down before anything starts.** A short document with deliverables, timeline, and a fixed price. You approve it. Then the work happens. No scope creep, no surprise invoices.

3. **You can tell whether it worked.** Every engagement ends with something concrete — a running app in the App Store, a working integration, a written recommendation you can actually act on. Not a deck of slides that justify another engagement.

This is unglamorous in a way that the consulting industry doesn't love, because the industry makes more money on long ambiguous engagements than on short crisp ones. But it's what most small businesses actually need.

## Who I'm building this for

The clearest way to describe the fit is by counter-example. 002 isn't a great match if you need:

- **An ongoing managed services relationship.** If your IT person is supposed to pick up the phone when something breaks on a Saturday, you need an MSP, not me.
- **A staff augmentation role on an existing team.** If your engineering org has five iOS devs and wants a sixth for six months, there are better options.
- **A "fractional CTO" on a long retainer.** Some people genuinely need that. It's not what I do.

It *is* a great match if you're:

- A small business that needs senior help on a specific thing, not an ongoing relationship
- A founder shipping an iOS app and wanting one person to own it end-to-end
- A team wondering whether some AI-assisted workflow would actually save time
- Someone about to commit to a big technical decision and wanting an independent read first
